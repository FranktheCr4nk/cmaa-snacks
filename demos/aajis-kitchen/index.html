Meet Seema · the Maa in Cmaa

The hands behind every spiral.

For thirty years, Seema has made faral for her family every Diwali — kneading bhajani by feel, pressing each chakli through the brass soncha, listening for the exact hiss that means the ghee is ready.

No recipe card, just memory in her fingertips. The steel paraat, the warm window light, the smell that pulled the whole house into the kitchen. That same batch — the one her children fought over — is now pressed fresh and packed for you, with a packed-on date, never a factory in sight.

Why Homemade Tastes Different

No machines. Just Maa's method.

Every tin walks through the same four steps Seema has used for three decades — no automation, no compromises.

01

Stone-ground bhajani

Rice and roasted dals ground fresh into our family bhajani flour — the soul of a real chakli, never a packet mix.

02

Kneaded & pressed by hand

Dough rested by feel, then pressed spiral by spiral through the brass soncha. Slower, yes — but you can taste the difference.

03

Fried fresh in pure ghee

Small batches into hot ghee, watched to a deep gold. No palm oil, no preservatives, no day-old stock.

04

Packed with a date

Cooled, sealed airtight and stamped with a packed-on date the same week it leaves the kitchen.

If Maa wouldn't serve it to her own family, it never reaches your tin.
