In Seema’s kitchen

Meet the Maa behind Cmaa

Cmaa sounds like “Seema,” and holds the word Maa. That is the whole idea — snacks made the way a mother makes them.

For thirty years Seema pressed chakli for festivals, for neighbours, for anyone who asked twice. The dough was always bhajani she roasted herself; the ghee always fresh; the spirals always turned by hand, never a machine.

Today that same kitchen ships across India and to families abroad who miss the taste of home. Nothing has changed except the address on the tin. Small batches. Real ghee. Pressed the day it leaves.

Why Homemade

No machines.
No shortcuts.
No apologies.

Everything that makes a snack feel mass-made, we left out. What remains is the part you actually taste.

01

Hand-pressed

Every spiral is turned by hand on a wooden press — not extruded by a factory line. It is slower. It is the point.

02

Small batch

We cook in kilos, not tonnes. A batch is finished, rested and tinned before the next one begins.

03

Zero preservatives

No additives, no anti-caking agents, no colours. Just bhajani, ghee, salt and spice — the way Maa keeps it.

04

Fresh cow ghee

Real ghee, never palm oil or vanaspati. It is what gives chakli that clean, melt-on-the-tongue snap.

05

Packed-on date

Every tin is stamped with the day it was made. We do not stockpile crunch — you get it fresh.

06

FSSAI certified

A home kitchen held to a commercial standard. Licensed, inspected, and proud to print the number.
