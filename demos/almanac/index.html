Chapter One · Meet the Maker

This is Seema.
You may call her Maa.

The name Cmaa hides two truths — it sounds like Seema, and it carries Maa. Everything that follows was made by her hands, in her kitchen, for yours.

i · the kitchen

Before the city wakes, a stove is lit.

Long before the first courier knocks, Seema is already at the counter — apron tied, ghee warming, a steel tin waiting to be filled. Her kitchen is small, the way real kitchens are, and it smells of roasted gram and patience. This is where Cmaa begins each morning: not in a factory, but at the edge of a home.

ii · the hands

Two hands that refuse to hurry.

She grinds her own bhajani — rice, chana, urad, a whisper of cumin — and roasts it slow. Each spice is measured by memory, not machine. Where a factory presses a thousand a minute, Seema presses a few hundred a day, each spiral coaxed from the press by the same hands that once braided hair and wiped tears.

iii · the recipe

A recipe with no shortcut.

There is no preservative in a Cmaa tin, because there is no recipe for one in her book. The chakli is fried in small batches, drained, cooled on cloth, and tasted — always tasted — before it is allowed to leave. If it would not be good enough for her own children, it does not go in the tin.

iv · the ritual

Made to be shared, over chai.

A chakli is never really eaten alone. It arrives with the kettle, with the visiting aunt, with the festival lamp. Cmaa exists for that small ritual — the snap of something crisp, the steam of masala chai, the feeling of being looked after. We pack a little of that home into every tin we send.

Chapter Three · Why Homemade

The slow way is
the only way.

Four steps, repeated every morning, that a machine could never be taught.

01

Grind & roast

Rice, chana and urad ground into fresh bhajani, then roasted slow until the kitchen turns golden.

02

Knead by hand

Warm ghee folded through by palm — never a mixer — until the dough remembers the shape of a spiral.

03

Press & fry

Pressed one tin at a time and fried in small batches so every chakli stays even, light and crisp.

04

Cool & tin

Cooled on cloth, tasted, and sealed the same day. No preservatives, because there's no need.
