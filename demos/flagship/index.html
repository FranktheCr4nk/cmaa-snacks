04:30 — SOAK & GRIND

Soak & grind

Rice, chana, urad — soaked overnight, ground while the street is still dark. Fresh bhajani smells like rain landing on a hot stone.

02
05:10 — KNEAD

Knead

Hot water, a spoon of ghee, and Seema’s knuckles. The dough is ready when it stops sticking and starts listening.

03
05:40 — PRESS

Press the spirals

One steady wrist-turn per chakli. Machines press circles; hands press spirals with a beginning and an end.

04
06:15 — GHEE-FRY

Ghee-fry & drain

Into ghee that is hot but never angry. Out at the first deep gold, onto old newspaper — the way it has always been done.

02

The spiral is pressed, never poured.

the boss, 4:55 AM

I started with one kadhai and my mother’s bhajani recipe, written in Marathi on the back of a calendar page. Every batch still begins the same way — I taste the flour before I knead it. If the sesame is even a little bitter, it goes back to the shop. People ask why I don’t make more. Because at forty tins a morning, I still know whose tin is whose. Yours has my name on it — and I mean that literally. Check the lid.

— Seema
