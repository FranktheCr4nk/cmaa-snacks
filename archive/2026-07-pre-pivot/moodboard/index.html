02 Identity

Four logo directions — built as one system

The recommendation isn't a single logo, it's a kit: a flexible wordmark for everyday, a chakli-spiral seal for craft, and a portrait mark so a real person signs every pack. Name + craft + person.

★ Primary — Wordmark
Seema's
Homemade Chakli & Chivda

"Seema's" in Fraunces, the apostrophe rendered as a tiny chakli-spiral or sesame seed. Warm, legible at favicon size, timeless — the safe master logo.

Seal — Chakli-spiral "S"

The coil of the S literally forms a chakli spiral. Works as a wax-seal, foil emboss, packaging tape, social avatar, app icon.

Emblem — Trust badge

The "small-batch craft" seal for labels, gift packs & stickers. Instant artisanal credibility — complements the wordmark, doesn't replace it.

Signature — Seema portrait

A warm, specific line portrait of Seema — bindi, hair tied back, a knowing half-smile. The "made by a real person" sticker on every pack & the About page. Humanises the brand like nothing else.

Seema's

A hand-signed flourish

A Caveat signature "Seema's" used only as a graphic moment — a signed label sticker, a sign-off on the thank-you card. Never for body text. It says: a person, not a corporation.

01 · Warm nostalgic homemade
Made the way Aai makes it.
Small-batch chakli & chivda, fried fresh in ghee, packed with love. — from Seema's kitchen
Taste Home →

Aaji's Kitchen ★ recommended base

Walking into your grandmother's kitchen on a Sunday morning.

Type: Fraunces + Mukta, Caveat for handwritten notes.

Imagery: soft window light, a mother's hands shaping spirals, steel paraat, brass plate, gentle imperfection.

Motion: gentle & warm — slow fades, soft parallax, steam drifting, content that "settles" like resting dough.

Emotion: trust, comfort, belonging — collapses the distance between a packet and a homemade gift.

Wins — when the sell is authenticity & emotional gifting. Exactly Seema's story.
Risk — "homemade" can read "amateur" if photography & layout aren't disciplined.

Refs: Paper Boat, Forest Essentials, Two Brothers Organic.

02 · Modern premium minimal
The humble, elevated.
Chakli & chivda. Handmade. Nothing else.

Quiet Luxury ★ discipline layer

Restraint as a flex — the snack floating like jewellery.

Type: Cormorant Garamond / Playfair + Inter.

Imagery: studio precision, single product, deep clean shadow, vast empty frame, expensive grade.

Motion: crisp & premium — letter-by-letter fades, slow scale-on-scroll, silence between moves.

Emotion: aspiration & self-assurance — "I send the tasteful gift."

Wins — premium gifting, corporate hampers, higher price point.
Risk — can feel cold / "not festive enough" and strip out Seema's warmth.

Refs: Awwwards Minimal, The Whole Truth, Aesop.

Roshni Bazaar ★ Diwali skin

Diwali, maxed out — diyas, marigolds, rangoli, abundance.

Type: Yatra One / Modak + Poppins.

Imagery: diya bokeh, brass thalis, marigold petals, glowing gift boxes.

Brown Paper & Twine

Kraft paper, rubber stamps, jute string, "batch no. 07."

Type: Space Mono / DM Mono + Work Sans.

Imagery: matte daylight, ingredients laid bare, hands wrapping & stamping, documentary "process" feel.

Motion: honest & tactile — stamp "thunk" reveals, parcel slide-ins, string that ties on hover.

Emotion: trust through transparency — "I see exactly what this is and who made it."

Wins — clean-ingredient, small-batch story for a younger urban buyer.
Risk — kraft is crowded; needs an ownable twist; under-festive.

Refs: DORIANS kraft, Two Brothers Organic.

The Seema Almanac

A beautifully art-directed food publication telling Seema's story.

Type: Marcellus / Spectral + Tiro Devanagari.

Imagery: editorial sequences — maker → ingredients → process → sharing; warm filmic grade.

Motion: editorial pacing — pinned chapters, choreographed text + image, "turning the page."

Emotion: depth & respect — "a real story, a real person, craft worth knowing."

Wins — PR, long-term depth, premium positioning.
Risk — story-heavy can bury the "buy" path; higher production cost.

Refs: Paper Boat, Awwwards Editorial, Kama Ayurveda.

My recommendation: build the core brand as Aaji's Kitchen executed with the Quiet Luxury discipline — the truest "premium-yet-warm, made-by-a-mother" expression — then ship Roshni Bazaar as a switchable Diwali skin (Sept–Nov). Fold Seema Almanac storytelling in as a "Seema's story" chapter. Warmth + credibility year-round, full festive firepower exactly when buyers shop.

10 How we build it

The stack & the ordering plan

Mobile-first, animation-ready, and order-taking — built on tooling we fully own. No Supabase, no Vercel, no Render, per your call.

Recommended stack

Next.js (App Router) TypeScript Tailwind CSS GSAP + ScrollTrigger Lenis smooth-scroll Framer Motion Embla carousels Razorpay (UPI/cards/COD) Shiprocket Cloudflare Pages / Netlify / VPS hosting
Supabase Vercel Render
Phase 1 · MVP

Marketing site + simple order

The full animated experience + catalog. Orders via Razorpay Payment Links & WhatsApp — zero backend. Ship fast.

Phase 2 · Shop

Real cart + checkout

On-site cart, Razorpay Checkout, a self-hostable data layer (PocketBase / MySQL on a VPS — not a BaaS) for products, orders & inventory + a light admin.

Phase 3 · Scale

Ops & growth

Inventory, Shiprocket automation, festive hamper builder, analytics (GA4 / Plausible), subscriptions & corporate gifting.

Mobile-first

Sticky thumb-zone add-to-cart, pin-code delivery check, one-page UPI/COD checkout, animations gated on prefers-reduced-motion & tested on mid-range Android.

Performance

Animate only transform/opacity, lazy-load below the fold, responsive images, code-split the heavy motion. Core Web Vitals green.

Compliance

FSSAI Basic Registration + GST sorted before go-live; FSSAI number on pack & footer; honest labelling.
