Anatomy of the label

Read the pouch like a selvedge.

Every pouch inside the tin carries its own collectible label — borrowed from the loom, not the supermarket shelf.

Hand-set Devanagari

The name is painted in sign-painter’s Yatra One — भाजणी चकली — pressed into the paper like a shop board on Laxmi Road.

Die-cut window

A cusped Maratha arch, cut clean through the label. You see the very batch you are buying — not a photograph of a better one.

Batch stamped

SB-11 · packed 02·07·26 · no preservatives. The footer is inked the morning it leaves the kadhai, never before.

The gifting ritual

Three gestures. One heirloom.

०१You reserve, we begin.

No warehouse, no stock. Your reservation starts a batch — bhajani roasted, dough rested, spirals pressed the week your dabba ships.

०२Six pouches, set like stones.

Each snack sealed in its labelled pouch, laid into the black tin on ivory paper, the wrap band folded the way a Paithani is folded — selvedge out.

०३Sealed in magenta, sent with a name.

A wax seal in silk magenta, a handwritten card in Seema aaji’s Marathi. Tell us who it is for; she writes to them, not to “the customer.”
