Chapter I

The Method

भाजणी, तूप, आणि सबुरी

Every almanac begins with a table of certainties. Ours has one entry: at 5:04 in the morning, in a two-burner kitchen in Kothrud, Seema aaji lights the stove and the year begins again. The bhajani1 was ground the previous evening — rice, chana, urad, a conspiracy of cumin — and it has rested overnight, the way all serious things rest.

The dough takes hot ghee the way paper takes ink: completely, and only once. She kneads until her knuckles report that it is done; no thermometer has ever been consulted, none has ever been needed. Then the sorya2 is loaded, the star die is seated, and two slow clockwise turns press the year's first spiral onto a cotton cloth.

What follows is arithmetic, not romance: forty spirals to a batch,3 one batch to a kadhai, and not one spiral fried before the oil says so. The method does not scale. That is the method.

  1. Grind & roast. Bhajani milled fresh, roasted till the kitchen smells like October.
  2. Knead with hot ghee. By palm, never paddle — the dough must remember a hand.
  3. Press. Two turns of the sorya, clockwise; the spiral lands in one breath.
  4. Fry, drain, tin. Small batches, cooled on cloth, sealed the same day.
N.B.Bhajani proportions, as recited: rice 4, chana 2, urad 1, coriander & cumin to the aaji's eye. Written down here for the first time.
ErrataCorrections to Issue 01: none. Aaji does not revise.
Field noteThe first spiral of every batch is hers to taste. This is not quality control. It is rank.

1. bhajani — the roasted multi-flour base of chakli; each house guards its ratio like a horoscope.

2. sorya — the hand-cranked brass press, fig. 1 on the cover; Seema's was part of her wedding trousseau, 1987.

3. forty, because the kadhai holds forty. The kadhai is older than the reader.

The Season Wheel

How to read this wheel

The year turns.
The kadhai follows.

Turn the page (scroll) and the wheel turns with you — twelve Marathi months, चैत्र through फाल्गुन, each with its ordained snack. The needle never moves; the year does.
