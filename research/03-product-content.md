## 1. PRODUCT DEEP-DIVE

### 1.1 What is Chakli?

**Chakli** (Marathi/Hindi: चकली; also **chakri**, **chakuli**) is a deep-fried, spiral-shaped savoury snack with a crisp, layered crunch. It is the western- and central-Indian cousin of the South Indian **murukku** (Tamil: *muruku*, meaning "twisted") and Telugu/Kannada **chakralu/chakkuli**.

- **Shape & name:** The defining feature is the tight, coiled spiral, traditionally piped through a hand-press called a **chakli press / sancha / murukku achu** fitted with a star-shaped die. The spiral is what gives chakli its name and its signature ridged, flaky bite.
- **Core difference from murukku:** Murukku is typically made from **rice flour + urad dal flour** (lighter, simpler). Chakli adds **chickpea/gram (chana) flour** and often a multi-grain roasted blend, plus a more complex spice mix, and uses the steel coiling press. This makes chakli denser, more savoury, and more aromatic.

**Regional varieties:**
- **Bhajani Chakli (Maharashtra)** — the gold standard; made from *bhajani*, a roasted multi-grain flour blend.
- **Rice-flour Chakli (Gujarat)** — lighter, often with sesame and ajwain; sometimes called *chakri*.
- **Butter Chakli (Karnataka)** — enriched with butter for a melt-in-mouth, less-spicy bite.
- **Murukku (Tamil Nadu / South)** — rice + urad, light and crunchy, sometimes coconut milk.
- **Multigrain / Ragi / Oats Chakli** — modern "healthier" variants.

### 1.2 What is Bhajani? (the heart of authentic chakli)

**Bhajani** is a Marathi word for a **roasted-and-ground multi-grain flour mix**. Traditional bhajani is made by dry-roasting and milling a blend of grains and dals, typically:

- **Grains:** rice, jowar (sorghum), bajra (pearl millet), wheat, and poha (flattened rice)
- **Dals/pulses:** chana dal (Bengal gram), urad dal, sometimes moong/masoor
- **Whole spices roasted in:** coriander seeds, cumin, carom (ajwain), fenugreek (methi), dried red chillies

The roasting deepens flavour (nuttiness) and improves shelf life and crispness. Bhajani is what separates an authentic, layered, aromatic Maharashtrian chakli from a plain rice-flour one.

### 1.3 How chakli is traditionally made

1. Roasted **bhajani flour** is mixed with hot oil/ghee "moyan" (shortening), salt, turmeric, red chilli, asafoetida (hing), sesame (til), ajwain, and a green chilli–ginger paste.
2. Bound into a soft, pliable dough with warm water (sometimes buttermilk).
3. Piped through the **chakli press** directly into hot oil as tight spirals.
4. **Deep-fried** on medium heat until golden and crisp throughout (low-and-slow is key — too hot and the inside stays soft).
5. Cooled fully before airtight storage to lock in the crunch.

### 1.4 What is Chivda?

**Chivda** (also **chiwda**, **chevda**, **poha namkeen**, part of the **farsan/faraal** family) is a savoury, ready-to-eat snack mix. The classic Maharashtrian/Gujarati version is built on **poha (flattened rice)** that is either deep-fried or **roasted**, then tossed with:

- **Peanuts, roasted chana (dalia), cashews**
- **Curry leaves, mustard & cumin seeds** (tempering / tadka)
- **Dry coconut slivers, raisins**
- **Turmeric, asafoetida, red chilli, salt, sometimes a touch of sugar/jaggery**

**Origin story:** Chivda emerged as a clever, thrifty way to **preserve and use grains** (notably rice flattened into poha) and prevent wastage in western Indian households. It became a staple festive and tea-time snack across Maharashtra and Gujarat.

**Regional spectrum:**
- **Maharashtrian poha chivda** — savoury, curry-leaf forward, balanced heat.
- **Gujarati chivda** — sweet-salty, with jaggery, dry fruit and a milder spice.
- **Rajasthani chivda** — spicier, heavy on nuts and dry fruit.
- **South Indian mixture** — mustard, curry leaf, tamarind tang, sev/boondi added.
- **Diet/Roasted (patal) poha chivda** — thin poha, roasted not fried, lighter on oil.

### 1.5 Cultural & festival significance (Diwali / Faral)

Both snacks are pillars of **Diwali Faral / Faraal** — the assortment of homemade festive snacks prepared in large batches in Maharashtrian and Gujarati homes and shared with family, neighbours and guests as a gesture of **abundance, prosperity and goodwill**. A classic faral platter pairs **chakli + chivda** with **shankarpali, chakli, karanji, besan ladoo, and bhakarwadi**.

The snacks are also year-round tea-time, travel, and tiffin staples — not just festival items. This dual identity (festive gifting **and** everyday munching) is a key marketing lever for Seema.

## 4. NUTRITION & TRUST (Honest positioning)

### Trust pillars
- **Traditional recipe** — authentic roasted bhajani and curry-leaf tempering, no shortcuts.
- **Quality oil** — fresh, clean frying oil (specify: groundnut/sunflower/rice-bran); oil changed regularly, never reused to darkness.
- **No preservatives, no artificial colours/flavours** — which is exactly why we sell fresh, small-batch, and date-stamp every pack.
- **Hygienically handmade** — clean home/cloud kitchen, gloved handling, sealed packaging.
- **FSSAI registered/licensed** — display your **FSSAI licence number** on the pack and footer (mandatory for selling packaged food in India; builds instant trust). Add nutritional information per 100g and an ingredient declaration as per FSSAI labelling norms.

### Talking about indulgence honestly
Don't over-claim "healthy" on deep-fried chakli. Be warm and honest:

> **A treat, made honestly.** Chakli and chivda are festive indulgences — and we keep them real: good grains, clean oil, real spices, nothing artificial. Our **Roasted Diet Chivda** is our lighter, roasted-not-fried option for everyday snacking, while our fried classics are the crunch you save for chai, guests and good company. Enjoy mindfully, share generously.

Honest, specific claims you *can* make:
- *Roasted (not fried)* — only for the Diet Chivda line.
- *Multigrain / made with millets (jowar, bajra)* — for bhajani products.
- *No added preservatives / no artificial colours* — if true for every SKU.
- *Source of plant protein & fibre* — chana dal, peanuts (keep modest, cite per-100g).

> Avoid: "guilt-free," "zero-calorie," or medical claims. Keep it credible.

### 6.2 FAQ (12, with answers)

**1. How fresh are your chakli and chivda?**
Every batch is hand-made fresh in small quantities and shipped soon after frying/roasting. We date-stamp each pack with a "Packed on" date and recommend enjoying within 30 days for peak crunch.

**2. Do your products contain preservatives or artificial colours?**
No. We use no preservatives, no artificial colours, and no artificial flavours — just real grains, clean oil and traditional spices. That's why we sell in small batches and ask you to store airtight.

**3. What is the shelf life and how should I store it?**
Chakli stays best for about 2–3 months and chivda for 3–6 months when sealed. Store in an airtight container in a cool, dry place away from sunlight and moisture. Always re-seal after opening.

**4. How do you ship and how long does delivery take?**
We ship pan-India via reliable courier partners in protective, sealed packaging. Orders are typically dispatched within 1–3 business days; delivery takes 3–7 days depending on location. Tracking is shared on dispatch.

**5. Will my snacks arrive crushed?**
We pack in sturdy, cushioned, airtight packaging designed to protect the spirals and crunch in transit. If anything arrives damaged, contact us with a photo and we'll make it right.

**6. What oil do you fry in?**
We use fresh, good-quality cooking oil (specify your oil), changed regularly and never reused to darkness. Our Roasted Diet Chivda is roasted, not fried, for a lighter option.

**7. Are your products vegetarian? Any allergens?**
All products are 100% vegetarian. They contain peanuts, tree nuts (cashew), sesame, wheat/gluten and may contain traces of other nuts — please check each product's ingredient list if you have allergies. Butter Chakli contains dairy.

**8. Can I choose the spice level?**
Many SKUs come in mild, medium and spicy. For gift boxes and bulk orders, we can customise the spice mix — just leave a note at checkout or contact us.

**9. Do you offer customisation or personalised gift notes?**
Yes. Festive and gift boxes can include a personalised message and custom spice selection. For corporate orders we offer custom branding and sleeves.

**10. Do you take bulk or corporate gifting orders?**
Absolutely. We offer bulk and corporate Diwali/festive hampers with custom branding, mix-and-match SKUs, and scheduled delivery. Contact us for MOQ and pricing — order early during festive season.

**11. Are you FSSAI certified?**

**12. Is this really homemade, or factory-made?**
