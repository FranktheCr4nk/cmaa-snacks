# Seema — Art Direction Mood Boards

**Brand:** Seema — homemade Chakli & Chivda, made by a mother. Authentic, premium-yet-warm. Indian market, festive/Diwali gifting, mobile-first, animation-rich.

**Purpose of this document:** Give the client SIX genuinely distinct visual directions to react to *before* we build. Each is a complete "world" — color, type, photography, texture, motion, hero — grounded in real reference brands. Pick one (or a hybrid of two) and we lock the design system.

**How to read this:** Don't evaluate these in isolation. Imagine each one on your phone, scrolling, the day before Diwali, with someone deciding whether to send a Seema box to their in-laws. Which one makes them feel *"this is special, and it's made with love"*?

## 1. Aaji's Kitchen
### *Warm nostalgic homemade — earthy, handwritten, made-by-mum*

**Mood / feeling & customer emotion**
The feeling of walking into your grandmother's kitchen on a Sunday morning — steam, the smell of frying, a steel dabba being opened. Targets **trust, comfort, and belonging**. The customer thinks: *"This tastes like home. This was made by hands, not a factory."* The emotional job here is to collapse the distance between a packaged product and a homemade gift.

**Color palette**
| Hex | Role |
|------|------|
| `#FBF4E6` | Background — warm cream / ghee |
| `#C8612D` | Primary — terracotta / fried-golden brown |
| `#7A3B1E` | Deep accent — roasted, masala brown |
| `#3E5C2F` | Secondary — curry-leaf green |
| `#D9A441` | Highlight — turmeric / haldi gold |
| `#4A3B2A` | Text — soft espresso (never pure black) |

**Typography**
- **Heading:** *Fraunces* (soft, optical serif with old-style warmth) or *Bitter*.
- **Accent / signature:** *Caveat* or *Gloria Hallelujah* for handwritten labels ("Seema's special", quantities, recipe notes).
- **Body:** *Mukta* or *Hind* (excellent Devanagari + Latin pairing, clean at small sizes).
- **Feel:** like handwriting on a recipe card slipped into a cookbook.

**Imagery / photography**
Soft natural window light, warm white balance, gentle shadows. Real hands: a mother's hands shaping chakli dough into spirals, oil shimmering in a kadhai, chivda being mixed in a steel paraat. Backgrounds of worn wooden boards, a cotton saree pallu, a brass plate, a kitchen towel. Macro crunch/texture shots. Slight imperfection is the point — no sterile studio.

**Texture / graphic elements**
Recycled-paper grain, faint flour dusting, hand-drawn doodles of spices and curry leaves, a subtle recipe-card lined-paper motif, torn-edge dividers. Tiny illustrated chakli spirals as bullets/loaders.

**Motion personality**
Gentle and warm. Slow fades, soft parallax, content that "settles" into place like dough resting. Steam wisps drift on the hero. Nothing snappy or aggressive — everything breathes. Easing: long, ease-out, organic.

**Hero concept**
Full-bleed warm photo of hands placing a fresh chakli on a steel plate, steam rising. Headline (handwritten + serif mix): **"Made the way Aai makes it."** Subhead: *Small-batch chakli & chivda, fried fresh in ghee, packed with love.* Soft CTA "Taste Home". Layout: single column, image-forward, generous breathing room.

**Reference sites / brands**
- **Paper Boat** — the gold standard for nostalgia-as-product in India; hand-drawn everyday-Indian scenes and memory-driven storytelling ([thehardcopy.co](https://thehardcopy.co/paperboat-and-the-taste-of-memories/)).
- **Forest Essentials** — warm heritage luxury, soft cream-and-gold palette that says "premium but rooted" ([vervemagazine.in](https://www.vervemagazine.in/food/indias-food-brands-are-drawing-up-an-appetite)).
- **Local/artisanal pickle & ghee brands** (e.g. Two Brothers Organic, Nutty Yogi) — kitchen-process photography and "made by a family" tone.

**Best for / risk**
*Wins* when the core sell is authenticity and emotional gifting — exactly Seema's story. *Risk:* if executed cheaply it can read as "homemade = unprofessional/unhygienic." Must keep photography crisp and the layout disciplined so "warm" never tips into "amateur."

## 2. Quiet Luxury
### *Modern premium minimal — editorial whitespace, the snack as a precious object*

**Mood / feeling & customer emotion**
Restraint as a flex. Lots of negative space, the product floating like jewellery. Targets **aspiration and self-assurance** — *"I send the tasteful gift. This brand has taste so I don't have to explain myself."* Reframes humble snacks as a considered, premium object.

**Color palette**
| Hex | Role |
|------|------|
| `#F4F1EC` | Background — bone / paper white |
| `#1A1A18` | Text — near-black ink |
| `#9A7B4F` | Primary accent — antique brass |
| `#E8E2D6` | Section tint — warm grey-beige |
| `#5B5B52` | Muted — stone grey |
| `#B8442C` | Single pop — used sparingly (CTA, festive moments) |

**Typography**
- **Heading:** *Cormorant Garamond* or *Playfair Display* — high-contrast editorial serif, large and confident.
- **Body / UI:** *Inter* or *Hanken Grotesk* — neutral, precise grotesque.
- **Feel:** a fashion magazine cover meets a fine-foods catalogue.

**Imagery / photography**
Studio precision. Single product, single hard-but-controlled light, deep clean shadow on a textured neutral surface (handmade paper, raw plaster, linen). Lots of empty frame. Tight, almost scientific macro of one chakli's spiral geometry. Muted, desaturated, expensive color grade. Occasional editorial flat-lay of the gift box, shot top-down.

**Texture / graphic elements**
Almost none — that's the point. A fine hairline rule, a thin brass divider, subtle paper grain, generous margins. Maybe an embossed monogram "S". Type does the heavy lifting.

**Motion personality**
Crisp and premium. Restrained, exact micro-interactions; smooth slow scroll reveals; type that fades up letter-by-letter; images that scale a touch on scroll. Tight, confident easing. Silence between movements. Never bouncy.

**Hero concept**
Vast bone-white screen. One perfectly lit chakli, off-center. Oversized serif headline: **"The humble, elevated."** Tiny line beneath: *Chakli & chivda. Handmade. Nothing else.* Minimal nav, one understated CTA. Layout: asymmetric editorial grid, huge whitespace.

**Reference sites / brands**
- **Awwwards "Minimal" & "Editorial Layout" collections** — black-and-white/neutral palettes, oversized type, understated motion define the 2025 premium look ([awwwards.com/minimal](https://www.awwwards.com/awwwards/collections/minimal/), [editorial-layout](https://www.awwwards.com/inspiration/editorial-layout)).
- **The Whole Truth** — proof that a *clean, typography-led* approach reads as premium and trustworthy in Indian FMCG ([thoughtoverdesign.com](https://thoughtoverdesign.com/work/thewholetruth)).
- **Aesop / high-end chocolatiers** — product-as-object minimalism with warm neutrals.

**Best for / risk**
*Wins* for premium gifting, corporate Diwali hampers, and a higher price point. *Risk:* can feel cold or "not Indian / not festive enough," and strips out the warmth that is Seema's actual differentiator. The single warm pop color and tactile paper textures must carry the soul, or it reads generic.

## 3. Roshni Bazaar
### *Festive vibrant bazaar — jewel tones, rangoli, full Diwali celebration*

**Mood / feeling & customer emotion**
Diwali, maxed out — diyas, marigolds, rangoli, fairy lights, abundance. Targets **joy, festivity, and generosity**. *"This is celebration in a box."* Built to convert hard in Sept–Nov gifting season and feel like a festival the moment the page loads.

**Color palette**
| Hex | Role |
|------|------|
| `#3A0B45` | Background — deep aubergine / night-sky purple |
| `#E63946` | Primary — kumkum red |
| `#F6A21E` | Secondary — marigold orange |
| `#FFD24C` | Highlight — diya / gold flame |
| `#118A7E` | Jewel accent — peacock teal |
| `#FFF3D6` | Light surface — warm ivory for cards/text panels |

**Typography**
- **Heading:** *Yatra One* or *Modak* (display, Indian-inflected, festive) for big festive words; *Marcellus* for an elegant counterweight.
- **Body:** *Poppins* or *Mukta* — friendly, geometric, great Devanagari support.
- **Feel:** invitation card meets a beautifully lit bazaar stall.

**Imagery / photography**
Rich, warm, low-key festive lighting with bokeh from diyas and string lights. Product styled on brass thalis, surrounded by marigold petals, dry fruits, rangoli powder. Saturated, glowing, celebratory. Gift boxes tied with ribbon. People-in-celebration shots (hands exchanging the box).

**Texture / graphic elements**
Rangoli geometry as section dividers and backgrounds, mandala radial motifs, gold foil accents, paisley borders, torans across the top of sections, twinkling-light particles. Subtle gold-on-dark patterning.

**Motion personality**
Bold and playful, celebratory. Diya flames flicker, gold particles drift, rangoli patterns draw themselves on scroll, elements pop and scale in with a little bounce. Energetic but not chaotic. Confetti/sparkle micro-moments on add-to-cart.

**Hero concept**
Deep purple night-sky hero, glowing diyas, a Seema gift box center-lit. Animated rangoli draws itself around it. Headline in festive display: **"Is Diwali, ghar ka swaad bhejiye."** ("This Diwali, send the taste of home.") Bright CTA: "Shop Gift Boxes". Layout: centered, symmetrical, ornamental frame.

**Reference sites / brands**
- **Cadbury Celebrations** — immersive festive imagery/video and iconic owned color used to *own* the gifting occasion ([confetti.design FMCG roundup](https://www.confetti.design/blog/top-10-food-centric-website-designs-for-indian-fmcg-entrepreneurs)).
- **Sukhadias / Adarsh Sweets** — festive mithai gifting with ribbons, hampers, jewel palettes ([ellegourmet.in Diwali guide](https://ellegourmet.in/food/the-ultimate-diwali-gifting-guide-2025-10544578)).
- **thinKitchen Diwali hampers** — customizable, beautifully-packaged festive gifting flows ([thinkitchen.in](https://www.thinkitchen.in/blogs/latest/best-diwali-gift-hampers-2025-the-ultimate-gifting-pack)).

**Best for / risk**
*Wins* as a *seasonal skin* and conversion machine for Diwali. *Risk:* festive maximalism can feel generic/clichéd and dates fast; it doesn't suit the *year-round* identity (chakli & chivda are everyday too). Best deployed as a switchable festive theme over a calmer base, not the permanent brand.

## 4. Brown Paper & Twine
### *Earthy artisanal craft — kraft, stamps, small-batch, honest*

**Mood / feeling & customer emotion**
The honest, hand-finished feel of a small-batch maker: kraft paper, rubber stamps, jute string, "batch no. 07." Targets **trust through transparency and craft**. *"I can see exactly what this is and who made it — no factory gloss."* Wellness-adjacent, ingredient-proud, anti-mass-market.

**Color palette**
| Hex | Role |
|------|------|
| `#D8C4A5` | Background — kraft paper tan |
| `#6E4B2A` | Primary — dark cocoa brown (stamp ink) |
| `#2F2A24` | Text — charcoal |
| `#8C9A5B` | Secondary — sage / dried-herb green |
| `#C24E2C` | Accent — burnt sienna stamp red |
| `#F0E9D8` | Light surface — unbleached cream |

**Typography**
- **Heading:** *Space Mono* or *DM Mono* (stamped, ledger, "ingredient label" feel) paired with *Libre Franklin* for warmth.
- **Body:** *Work Sans* or *Karla* — clean, honest, unpretentious.
- **Feel:** an apothecary label / a maker's stamped craft tag.

**Imagery / photography**
Natural diffuse daylight, matte finish, no gloss. Product on kraft, jute, raw wood, slate. Visible ingredients laid out honestly (rice flour, sesame, peanuts, curry leaves). Hands wrapping, tying, stamping. Slightly grainy, documentary, "process" feel — show the making, not just the result.

**Texture / graphic elements**
Kraft-paper texture everywhere, rubber-stamp graphics (batch number, date, "handmade", weight), twine/string accents, hand-drawn ingredient illustrations, postal/parcel motifs, dotted-line cut marks. Mascot illustration option (a small line-art chakli character).

**Motion personality**
Honest and tactile, mid-tempo. Stamp "thunk" reveals, elements that slide in like a parcel being packed, paper-fold transitions, string that "ties" on hover. Friendly, grounded — not flashy, not sleepy.

**Hero concept**
Kraft-textured background, a parcel half-wrapped in brown paper and twine, stamped **"SEEMA · BATCH 07 · MADE BY HAND."** Headline (mono): **"No shortcuts. Just small batches."** Subhead lists the 5 ingredients, honestly. CTA "See how it's made." Layout: structured, label-like, left-aligned.

**Reference sites / brands**
- **DORIANS / kraft-illustration packaging** — kraft as identity with character illustrations and a unified maker's visual system ([bsflexpack snack design](https://www.bsflexpack.com/snack-packaging-design/)).
- **Two Brothers Organic Farms** — Indian small-batch, ingredient-honest, farm-to-jar storytelling.
- **Kin Euphorics-style "warm-material craft"** — industrial honesty softened by kraft + metallic accents, a current award-winning trend ([toptopmfg packaging roundup](https://toptopmfg.com/blog/50-best-food-packaging-designs-that-actually-work-in-2026/)).

**Best for / risk**
*Wins* if Seema leans into "clean ingredients, small batch, transparency" and a slightly younger urban buyer. *Risk:* kraft/craft is crowded and can read as generic "indie food" if not given an ownable twist (signature stamp, mascot, color). Less overtly festive — needs a plan for Diwali season.

## 5. Crunch!
### *Playful contemporary snack — bold type, big color, fun motion*

**Mood / feeling & customer emotion**
Loud, fun, confident — the snack *is* the personality. Big type, punchy color blocks, cheeky copy, springy motion. Targets **delight and shareability**. *"This is fun, modern, made-for-me — and the crunch is the whole point."* Pulls Seema toward a younger, snack-first, social-native audience.

**Color palette**
| Hex | Role |
|------|------|
| `#FFF8E1` | Background — bright warm cream |
| `#FF5630` | Primary — electric chilli orange-red |
| `#1B1B3A` | Text / contrast — deep ink navy |
| `#FFC93C` | Secondary — pop yellow |
| `#2DBE9A` | Accent — fresh mint green |
| `#FF8FB1` | Tertiary — playful pink |

**Typography**
- **Heading:** *Clash Display* / *Anton* / *Bricolage Grotesque* — huge, bold, attitude. Used at massive scale.
- **Body:** *Satoshi* or *Plus Jakarta Sans* — modern, friendly, geometric.
- **Feel:** confident poster typography you can read across the room.

**Imagery / photography**
High-key, punchy, color-blocked backgrounds. Product flying/floating, crumbs mid-air, dynamic "explosion of crunch." Bold drop shadows, playful scale (giant single chakli). Optionally cut-out product collaged onto flat color + illustration. Energetic, social-ready, scroll-stopping.

**Texture / graphic elements**
Flat bold shapes, squiggles, stars, halftone dots, sticker-style badges ("100% ghee", "0 maida"), big arrows, outline illustrations. Crumb/spice particle bursts. Chunky rounded UI.

**Motion personality**
Bold and playful — the loudest of the six. Springy, bouncy, overshoot easing. Things wobble, pop, spin, snap to a beat. Hover delights everywhere, scroll-triggered "crunch" sounds optional, sticker pop-ins, marquee tickers. High energy, mobile-first thumb-candy.

**Hero concept**
Big flat color hero, a giant chakli mid-spin with crumbs flying. Enormous headline: **"CRUNCH THAT MEANS IT."** Sub: *Homemade chakli & chivda. No maida. No nonsense.* Big rounded CTA "Grab a Pack". Layout: bold, full-bleed color, oversized type, asymmetric.

**Reference sites / brands**
- **The Whole Truth** — bold, casual, typography-led, "declare it in big font upfront" attitude; the Indian benchmark for this energy ([thoughtoverdesign.com](https://thoughtoverdesign.com/work/thewholetruth)).
- **Open Secret** — "India ki Un-Junk Dukaan," cheeky positioning, playful clean-label snack branding ([opensecret.in](https://opensecret.in/)).
- **Slurrp Farm** — bright, friendly, illustration-forward "made with love, no nasties" tone ([slurrpfarm via flipkart brand page](https://www.flipkart.com/food-products/slurrp-farm~brand/pr?sid=eat)).

**Best for / risk**
*Wins* for D2C, social/UGC, impulse snack buyers, and a younger audience. *Risk:* loud and playful can undercut the *premium gifting* and *"made by a mother, authentic"* equity — it may read as mass-snack, not heirloom. Hardest to make feel "special enough" to gift to elders.

## 6. The Seema Almanac
### *Heritage Indian editorial — magazine storytelling, regional motifs, sophisticated*

**Mood / feeling & customer emotion**
A beautifully art-directed food publication telling Seema's story — region, ritual, recipe, the maker. Targets **depth, respect, and emotional investment**. *"There's a real story and a real person here; this is craft worth knowing."* The most narrative, scroll-as-story experience.

**Color palette**
| Hex | Role |
|------|------|
| `#F3EDE1` | Background — aged paper ivory |
| `#7B1E1E` | Primary — deep maroon / sindoor |
| `#1F2A24` | Text — ink green-black |
| `#C58A3D` | Accent — old gold |
| `#3D5A4C` | Secondary — deep banana-leaf green |
| `#A8432A` | Highlight — terracotta rust |

**Typography**
- **Heading / display:** *Marcellus* or *Spectral* (refined, literary serif) with occasional *Tiro Devanagari* for authentic bilingual headlines.
- **Body:** *Spectral* or *Lora* for long-form readability; *Inter* for captions/UI.
- **Feel:** a sophisticated food magazine — Mrs Balbir Singh's cookbook reissued by a design studio.

**Imagery / photography**
Editorial food photography: considered compositions, directional warm light, deep tones, story sequences (portrait of the maker → ingredients → process → finished dish → people sharing). Documentary + still-life blend. Regional context: a Maharashtrian kitchen, a specific town, festival prep. Color grade: warm, rich, filmic.

**Texture / graphic elements**
Regional folk-art motifs used tastefully (Warli line figures, kolam/rangoli linework) as section markers and dividers. Drop caps, pull quotes, marginalia, footnotes, "chapter" numbering, hand-drawn maps, ledger rules. Restrained gold-foil-style accents.

**Motion personality**
Crisp and premium with editorial pacing. Story-driven scroll: text and image choreographed, pinned sections, slow horizontal "chapter" transitions, subtle parallax between foreground and background layers. Calm, deliberate, "turning the page." Sophisticated, never gimmicky.

**Hero concept**
Magazine cover energy: a striking portrait of the maker (or hands + chakli) with an elegant serif masthead. Headline: **"A spiral of flour, ghee and memory."** Kicker: *Issue 01 — The story of Seema's kitchen.* CTA "Read the story" / "Shop the collection." Layout: editorial grid, masthead, generous type hierarchy.

**Reference sites / brands**
- **Paper Boat** — story-first, comic-strip and festival narratives integrated with product, hand-drawn Indian scenes ([thehardcopy.co](https://thehardcopy.co/paperboat-and-the-taste-of-memories/)).
- **Awwwards Editorial Layout collection** — pinned, choreographed, magazine-style scroll storytelling as a premium device ([awwwards.com](https://www.awwwards.com/inspiration/editorial-layout)).
- **Forest Essentials / Kama Ayurveda** — heritage-as-luxury, regional authenticity rendered sophisticated, not kitschy ([vervemagazine.in](https://www.vervemagazine.in/food/indias-food-brands-are-drawing-up-an-appetite)).

**Best for / risk**
*Wins* when the founder story is genuinely compelling and the brand wants long-term depth, PR, and premium positioning. *Risk:* story-heavy sites can bury the "buy" path and feel slow; needs disciplined content (real photography, real copy) — it falls apart with stock imagery and thin storytelling. Higher production cost.

## MY RECOMMENDATION

For Seema, I'd push two directions — one as the **core brand**, one as a **seasonal layer**:

### Primary: **#1 Aaji's Kitchen** (with a disciplined, premium hand)
This is the truest expression of Seema's actual differentiator — *made by a mother, authentic, warm*. It directly serves the gifting emotion ("send the taste of home") that drives this category, and the warm-cream/terracotta/turmeric palette photographs beautifully for both everyday and festive contexts. The key is to execute it with **the restraint and crisp photography of #2 Quiet Luxury** so "homemade" never reads as "amateur." Borrow Quiet Luxury's whitespace, type discipline, and product-as-object shots; keep Aaji's Kitchen's soul, hands, and handwriting. This hybrid is "premium-yet-warm" — exactly the brief.

### Seasonal layer: **#3 Roshni Bazaar** (as a switchable Diwali skin)
Diwali gifting is where the money is. Rather than commit the whole year to festive maximalism (which dates and feels generic), build the base in the Aaji's/Quiet-Luxury hybrid and ship **Roshni Bazaar as a themeable festive overlay** (Sept–Nov): jewel tones, rangoli motifs, gift-box flows, celebratory motion. Best of both — warmth and credibility year-round, full festive firepower exactly when buyers are shopping.

**Why not the others as primary:** *Crunch! (#5)* is fun but risks cheapening the heirloom/gifting equity; *Brown Paper & Twine (#4)* is strong and ownable but crowded and under-festive; *The Seema Almanac (#6)* is gorgeous and the best long-term PR play, but story-heavy and costly — bring its storytelling *modules* into the Aaji's Kitchen build (a "Seema's story" chapter) rather than making the whole site a magazine.
