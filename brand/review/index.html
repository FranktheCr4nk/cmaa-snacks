Brand logo

Twelve concepts were generated and judged against the brief (flat vector, roast on cream, chakli-coil integration, bilingual सीमा, honesty ring). The top three were rebuilt as production-ready SVGs — real outlined Fraunces + Plex glyphs, mathematically computed chakli spirals. Those three are the real candidates below; the full L1–L12 exploration follows for the record.

02

Packaging

Three directions rendered photoreal against the campaign anchor. Every accepted render passed two gates: all label text legible and correctly spelled (Seema · सीमा · SKU · “no palm oil · no preservatives” · 250 g) and the food in the window obeys the anatomy law. Print-ready label artwork already exists for the winner.

“1st — D1 Loud color-block. The pouch is the world color, with nothing on it but cream Fraunces type and a die-cut window of the real food. It is literally the site; it owns its shelf meter; the window makes the food do the appetite work. 2nd — D3 the tin, as the gift/festive tier, not the everyday SKU. 3rd — D2 kraft, credible but the default artisan look — steal its batch stamp onto D1’s back panel.”

— packaging agent’s ranked verdict · brand/pack-round2/DIRECTIONS.md
P-D1

Loud color-block

Ranked 1st — the recommendation
  • It is literally the site. Turmeric, chili and curry-leaf pouches drop straight into the site’s color worlds — pack shots, heroes and social crops become one system.
  • Shelf physics. One flat saturated slab + giant cream serif owns the shelf; nothing in the Indian snacks aisle looks like it.
  • It shows the food. The die-cut window is the only “image” on the pack — the real chakli coil and poha flakes do the selling.

Already print-ready: 130 × 200 mm front panels, 3 mm bleed, all text outlined to paths, magenta dieline layer for the window cut. Only risk: pouch color match to the world hex needs one printed drawdown (matte lamination shifts saturation).

P-D3

The premium tin

Ranked 2nd — keep as the Diwali / gifting tier

Roast-brown tin, ghee-gold embossed spiral lid, cream band — the most premium object of all eleven renders. Unit economics don’t fit a ₹150–250 everyday snack, so it shouldn’t replace the pouch: run it seasonally as “Seema · The Tin.”

Deliberately steps outside the LOUD color worlds into roast + gold — right for festive, wrong for every day.

P-D2

Kraft honest

Ranked 3rd — credible, but the most generic

Kraft pouch, cream label, handwritten “batch 47 · packed by hand”, spiral rubber-stamp in SKU color. Truest to “mother-made” — but kraft + cream is the default artisan look of every farmers-market brand; on shelf it recedes exactly where D1 shouts. The batch stamp is worth stealing onto D1’s back panel.

All three render correctly (windows obey the anatomy law) — the objection is strategic, not craft.
