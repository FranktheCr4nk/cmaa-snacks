# Cmaa Logo Craft — Research Brief

Research round: July 2026. Purpose: inform selection + refinement of Cmaa's logo
(homemade Maharashtrian snacks; chakli-spiral motif; bilingual Latin + Devanagari).
Four planned families under evaluation: **wordmark / spiral mark / emblem / illustrative**.
The lab currently holds 6 options per family (A1–A6 wordmarks, B1–B6 spirals, C1–C6
emblems, D1–D6 illustrative), each in color / mono / reversed. This brief is written to
judge and refine *those specific* options.

---

## 1. Iconic food marks that scale (favicon ↔ storefront)

**The scale test is the first gate.** Practitioner consensus: test the mark at 16px,
32px, ~100–150px, ~400px, and signage size; convert to one-color black and white; the
master file must be vector. At 16×16 the favicon "can't afford" gradients, intricate
detail, or clever negative space — detail becomes noise. A useful diagnostic: blur the
mark; if it dissolves into visual noise, it lacks the visual mass to survive small sizes.
(Sources: [Logodesign.net scalability](https://www.logodesign.net/blog/logo-scalability/),
[Unwrite favicon best practices 2025](https://unwrite.co/blog/favicon-design-best-practices-2025/),
[Creaticca scalability across platforms](https://creaticca.com/logo-scalability-across-platforms/).)

**Responsive logo = a level-of-detail pipeline, designed up front.** The modern pattern
(Google, Warner Bros, Spotify) is a complex-to-minimal ladder: full lockup (storefront,
packaging hero) → mark + short wordmark (nav bars) → mark alone (favicon, app icon,
embroidery on aprons, wax seal / sticker). Each level is *drawn*, not just shrunk: strokes
thicken, counters open, detail sheds. Cmaa needs all four levels specified from day one
because the brand lives on a pouch roundel, a website nav, a WhatsApp DP, and a favicon
simultaneously. (Storefront demands legibility at distance; favicon demands bold shapes
and strong contrast — different jobs, one system.)

**Burger King 2021 (JKR) is the reference case for food-brand flatness.** They dropped
the glossy 3D swoosh for a flat modern redraw of the 1969 bun-logo — "radically simpler
yet familiar" — explicitly to be digital-friendly, and paired it with a proprietary
food-shaped font (Flame). Lesson for Cmaa: the mark should look like the *food category*
at a glance (chakli spiral = the "bun" of our system), flat, chunky, warm — no bevels,
no gradients. This directly indicts any lab option leaning on radial gradients or relief
shading (e.g. C3 Wax Seal's colour build) as the *primary* mark; those are dressed
derivatives, not the master.

**Amul: consistency compounds.** The Amul girl (Sylvester da Cunha + Eustace Fernandes,
1966) has never changed in ~60 years; the equity came from repetition + topical wit, not
redesign. Heritage Indian food brands earn trust through a *fixed* mark and a *variable*
voice. Cmaa should lock the mark early and put all play into copy, flavour colours, and
packaging — never into logo variants.

## 2. Modern Indian identity systems (the wave we're riding)

The It's Nice That "View From Mumbai" survey (Apr 2024) is the single best map of the
current wave. Its roster — and the specific, *researched* reference each brand mines —
is the standard Cmaa is being measured against:

| Brand | Studio | Specific cultural reference mined |
|---|---|---|
| Bombay Sweet Shop | Please See// | Art Deco South-Bombay architecture; Rani Bagh flora/fauna |
| Subko | Studio Bigfat | Vintage Bollywood tri-script posters; Paderu (AP) regional styles |
| Goenchi Feni | Studio Bigfat | Hand-drawn feni production; traditional *garrafão* glass carboys |
| Pattri | Thought Over Design | Indian Railways dual-tone trains + stencilled type; regional-snack watercolours |
| Kalkandu | — | Tanjore paintings; Athangudi tiles; Chennai hand-painted sweet-shop signs |
| Tāmras Gin | Irregulars Alliance | Greek lotus-eaters myth; Goan azulejo tiles |
| Terai Gin | Quick Brown Fox | Vintage family coins; carved architectural pillars; Channapatna toy craft |

The common thread, in Aniruddh Mehta's words: **"some things are better re-imagined or
repurposed, rather than created new."** Cultural reference must be *specific and
researched*, never decorative paisley — identical to our brief rule #7.
(Sources: [It's Nice That — View From Mumbai](https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/the-view-from-mumbai-indian-packaging-graphic-design-300424).)

**Bombay Sweet Shop (Please See//)** re-frames mithai through "play" — maximalist
packaging, dazzling colour, Art Deco logo cues. But note the *logo itself* is disciplined
and architectural; the maximalism lives in the packaging and illustration *around* it.
This separation — **calm mark, loud world** — is the operating model for Cmaa and matches
our existing brief rule #1 ("loud pack, calm canvas").
(Sources: [Shweta Mulekar — BSS](https://shwetamulekar.com/Bombay-Sweet-Shop),
[Vidhi Doshi — BSS](https://www.vidhidoshi.com/bombay-sweet-shop).)

**Subko (Studio Bigfat)** is the bilingual benchmark. The name is a portmanteau —
Hindustani *"sabko"* ("for everyone / for all") re-spelled **Subko = "from the
subcontinent"** — and the wordmark unites **three scripts as equal citizens**: Latin
(English), Devanagari (Sanskritic languages), Nastaliq (Urdu), a "tri-script" lifted from
vintage Bollywood posters. Scripts are *equal citizens of the identity, not translations*;
Subko is recognisable from the script stack alone. It also chose a rigid **box** over
gusseted bags for tactility and shelf presence. Takeaway: **the bilingual lockup is itself
the brand asset.** For Cmaa this validates the A3 "Bilingual Twin" direction (CMAA over
चमा sharing one bar) as a first-class candidate, not a caption.
(Sources: [Studio Bigfat — Subko](https://studiobigfat.com/Subko-Specialty-Coffee-Roasters),
[The Hard Copy — Subko story](https://thehardcopy.co/branding-case-study-subko/),
[World Brand Design Society](https://worldbranddesign.com/studio-bigfat-branding-and-packaging-design-for-asias-new-specialty-coffee-roasters/).)

**Paper Boat (Elephant Design)** is built on a "limbic imprint" — the name comes from a
Jagjit Singh ghazal; the mark is a simple folded paper boat anyone can draw from memory.
Soft pastels, curvilinear hand-drawn forms, stories printed on packs. Lesson: **a mark
that encodes a memory beats a mark that encodes a product.** For Cmaa, the spiral
shouldn't merely say "chakli"; it should say "the sound of the sorya press in aaji's
kitchen" — i.e. pick the drawing style that feels hand-made, not die-cut.
(Sources: [Elephant Design — Paperboat](https://www.elephantdesign.com/services/branding-packaging/brand-strategy/paperboat),
[The Hard Copy](https://thehardcopy.co/paperboat-and-the-taste-of-memories/).)

**Blue Tokai** is the fixed-form / variable-colour benchmark. "Tokai" is Malabari for the
peacock's plume (Malabar = where colonists first cultivated Indian coffee), so the mark is
a **blue peacock plume in a circle**. Crucially, **the plume recolours per blend/SKU while
the circle-and-silhouette stay fixed** — and the packs carry rotating regional art forms
(Gond, Warli, Madhubani, Mandana). This maps exactly onto Cmaa's 4 flavour-keyed SKU
colours (bhajani maroon, butter ochre, masala red, poha green): **one silhouette, four
inks.** Any lab option whose recognition depends on its colour build (B4 Sesame Orbit's
gold→maroon gradient, C3's relief) is weaker here than options that hold as a pure
silhouette (B1, B6, A6).
(Sources: [Blue Tokai — what tokai means](https://bluetokaicoffee.com/blogs/blue-tokai-coffee-roasters/what-on-earth-does-tokai-mean),
[Brand Strategy Lab breakdown](https://thebrandstrategylab.com/blog/blue-tokai-brand-strategy-for-coffee-businesses/).)

**Amul heritage:** covered in §1 — the 60-year fixed mark is the argument for locking
Cmaa's mark now and pushing all variability into the mascot/voice layer.

**Fantome:** could not verify a published case study in this round (searches surfaced no
authoritative studio page or feature). Treat as **unverified**; do not cite it as
precedent. The seven brands in the table above are load-bearing and well-documented.

## 3. Spiral marks precedent — owning a spiral without reading generic

The spiral is *rare* among major brands, because it is read many ways (hypnosis,
dizziness, snail, "loading"). The ones that own it each **anchor the spiral to something
specific**:

- **Air New Zealand's koru:** a Māori word for "loop/coil," evoking a silver-fern frond
  unfurling — new life, growth, strength. Adopted around the 1973 DC-10 launch and still
  in use 50+ years later. It survives because it is a *culturally specific, named* spiral
  with a story, drawn with confident asymmetric weight and an inner coil that "returns to
  the point of origin" — **not** a geometric Archimedean coil. The airline: "its
  simplicity, its boldness, its elegance … we have been able to use it as the starting
  point for everything." ([Koru — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koru),
  [Stuff — the man behind the koru](https://www.stuff.co.nz/travel/350535648/revealed-the-man-and-the-story-behind-air-new-zealand-s-famous-koru),
  [Air NZ logo history](https://logos-world.net/air-new-zealand-logo/))
- **Ubisoft:** spiral-as-fingerprint/swirl — owns it via a gestural, hand-drawn line
  quality, minimal black-on-white, and negative-space play; the fingerprint reading signals
  "immersion/involvement." This is the direct precedent for Cmaa's **B6 "Pressed Thumb"**
  (spiral that double-reads as a thumbprint = hand-pressed identity). ([1000logos spiral
  roundup](https://logos-world.net/most-famous-logos-with-a-spiral/))
- **Cinnamon-roll shops:** the cautionary tale — hundreds of near-identical concentric
  swirls flood logo-maker galleries; a *generic* spiral is the single most template-looking
  mark in food. ([99designs spiral gallery](https://99designs.com/inspiration/logos/spiral))

**How Cmaa's spiral avoids genericism — the chakli is NOT a smooth spiral:**
1. **The ridges.** A chakli's defining feature is its spiked/ridged surface from the sorya
   (press). A spiral with a subtly serrated or beaded outer edge is instantly chakli, never
   cinnamon roll. (B1's "ridge grooves cut at equal arc spacing" is exactly this move.)
2. **The termination.** Real chaklis end in a tapered, slightly lifted tail where the dough
   was cut — an asymmetric, imperfect terminal reads handmade (Paper Boat logic).
3. **Squared spiral option.** Traditional Maharashtrian bhajani chakli coils slightly
   square/angular, not perfectly circular — an ownable geometry no logo-maker template has.
4. **Cultural anchoring.** Like the koru, *name* the form in the brand story (the sorya
   press, aaji's hand) so the spiral has a *why*, not just a shape.
5. **Negative-space double-read.** Strongest direction: a spiral that also reads as a
   letterform (the 'C' of Cmaa, or the counter of Devanagari च) or as a thumbprint — one
   drawing, two reads. (A1's C-counter spiral, A6's spiral ligature, B6's thumbprint.)

Local category check: existing chakli/murukku sellers (Haldiram's, Anand, Ghotaram, Sweet
Karam Coffee) use commodity packaging with photographic packs and generic script logos —
**no one in the category owns a crafted spiral mark.** The lane is open.

## 4. Bilingual lockup best practice (Devanagari + Latin)

Concrete rules from Alphabettes' "Devanagari Typography 101," Pooja Saxena's Devanagari
anatomy work, and multiscript practice
([Alphabettes](https://www.alphabettes.org/devanagari-typography-101-a-guide-for-typesetting-with-latin/),
[Devanagari anatomy — Pooja Saxena](https://www.type-together.com/devanagari-type-anatomy),
[Logodesign.net multilingual logos](https://www.logodesign.net/blog/typography-for-multilingual-logos/),
[Ek Type — Mukta Latin](https://ektype.in/mukta-latin.html)):

1. **Alignment — the shirorekha carries it, not the baseline.** Devanagari *hangs* from
   the shirorekha (headline). Optically place the shirorekha **between Latin small-cap
   height and cap-height** (the documented sweet spot); treat ukars and lower matras as
   descenders. Never force both scripts onto one mathematical baseline. A3's "shirorekha
   doubles as the Latin underline — one shared bar" is a legitimate, strong move *if* the
   bar lands in that optical zone.
2. **Optical sizing.** At equal point size Devanagari reads smaller and lighter next to
   Latin (especially ALL-CAPS Latin). Scale Devanagari up (≈8–15%, judged by eye) until the
   *typographic colour* — weight/texture — of the two lines matches.
3. **Stroke contrast — match amount, not direction.** Latin and Devanagari stress run in
   opposite directions. High-contrast serif Latin ↔ modulated Devanagari; low-contrast
   sans ↔ monolinear Devanagari. The thick junction where a curved stroke meets a vertical
   is a *feature* of high-contrast Devanagari — **never thin those junctions to imitate
   Latin stress** ("reversed-contrast," instantly amateur). Calibrate Latin 'o' against 'क'.
4. **Shirorekha integrity.** Don't letterspace so the headline breaks accidentally; don't
   bolt a fake shirorekha onto Latin letters ("Devanagarisation" — disrespectful and
   amateur). *Intentional* shirorekha play (curved, doubled, deliberately broken) is
   legitimate emphasis.
5. **Case.** Sentence-case or lowercase Latin sits better beside Devanagari than ALL CAPS,
   which reads too heavy against the hanging script (if caps are used, add point-size and a
   baseline shift to compensate).
6. **Which script leads — equal citizens, context-switched.** The Bollywood-poster / Subko
   model (scripts as equal stacked citizens) beats caption-style subordination. Global name
   drives *recognition*, local script builds *trust/accessibility*. For Cmaa (D2C, Instagram
   discovery, Marathi-core audience + pan-India shipping): **lead with Devanagari on
   packaging** (shelf/gift, emotional register — "चकली," "आजीचा फराळ") and **lead with Latin
   in digital UI** (nav, favicon, checkout). Design the lockup so *either* line can be
   dropped without breaking the composition.
7. **Custom over default — the #1 Indian-D2C failure.** A custom Latin logotype paired with
   a system/default Devanagari font is the most common tell of amateur work. Both scripts
   must get equal drawing effort. **Ek Type** (Mukta multiscript family, Mumbai) is the
   benchmark for harmonised Indic+Latin; use a lockup matrix/grid so proportions hold across
   scripts. Note: A3 already uses *real Yatra One* outlines for चमा — good; verify its
   weight/contrast is tuned to the Latin, not left at default.

## 5. Mark + wordmark system architecture, clearspace, one-color, motion

**Architecture (what Cmaa must specify):**
- **Primary lockup:** mark + bilingual wordmark (packaging front, site header, invoices).
- **Secondary lockup:** mark + Latin-only wordmark, horizontal (nav bars, email footer).
- **Mark alone:** only after co-exposure earns recognition; on day one it may stand alone
  in *supported* contexts (favicon, pouch roundel, sticker, watermark) where the name
  already appears nearby. Rule of thumb: **the mark stands alone where the container already
  says the brand.**
- **Clearspace:** define as an *internal* unit of the logo (e.g. one spiral-coil width, or
  the height of the Devanagari 'च') so the rule scales with the logo.
- **One-color discipline:** the mark must survive 1-colour ink (cream on maroon, ink on
  cream), reversed, embossed/foiled on the gift tin, and rubber-stamped on kraft. **Design
  the one-color version first;** colour is applied, not structural. This is where the emblem
  family (C1–C6) is most at risk — banners, misregistration plates, wax relief and postmark
  detail clog at small sizes.
- **Minimum sizes:** specify px + mm floors per lockup level (e.g. full bilingual lockup
  never below ~120px / ~30mm wide; below that, auto-switch down the responsive ladder).

**Motion behavior (award-site patterns, 2025–26):**
Motion identity systems now ship **four tiers**: full reveal (5–10s, brand film) → short
sting (1–2s, ads/reels) → **micro-animation** (website load / app moments: ONE small action
— "a line completes, a dot lands, a symbol aligns, a shape breathes once") → static fallback
(reduced-motion, email, print, thumbnails). "Quiet micro-motion is the antidote to
overproduced logo animation." Klarna's motion badge (used to open/close every video) is the
canonical single-asset example. For Cmaa's site the natural on-load micro is **the spiral
drawing itself in** (SVG `pathLength` dash-draw, ~600–900ms, `cubic-bezier(.2,.8,.2,1)`),
which also matches our v2 motion cookbook technique #3 — **the logo's motion *is* the site's
signature motion, one system.** Devanagari can resolve with a shirorekha wipe (headline
draws left→right) as the final beat. Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with the static
mark. B1/A6 (single continuous spiral path) are the easiest to animate this way; B6's
thumbprint can "press in"; the emblem family cannot micro-animate cleanly (too many parts).
(Sources: [everything.design motion guidelines 2026](https://www.everything.design/blog/motion-brand-guidelines),
[Renderforest logo animation trends 2026](https://www.renderforest.com/blog/logo-animation-trends),
[logodiffusion motion best practices](https://logodiffusion.com/blog/maintaining-brand-identity-in-logo-animations).)

## 10 evaluation criteria for judging the generated options

Score each option 1–5 per criterion; **kill anything scoring ≤2 on criteria 1–4** (these
are structural, not taste).

1. **16px survival.** Render at favicon size, 1-colour. Still unmistakably the mark? Blur
   test: does it hold mass or dissolve into noise?
2. **One-color structural integrity.** Works in flat ink-on-cream and cream-on-maroon with
   zero gradients/overlaps needed. Foil-, stamp-, embroidery-ready. (Design 1-colour first.)
3. **Chakli, not cinnamon roll.** Does the spiral carry a category-proofing feature —
   ridged/beaded edge, squared coil, tapered handmade terminal? Would it look *wrong* on a
   bakery? (It should.)
4. **Script parity.** Devanagari drawn to the same craft level as Latin (no system font);
   shirorekha in the Latin small-cap→cap-height zone; typographic colour matched line-to-line.
5. **Memory-drawability.** Can someone sketch it after one glance (Paper Boat / koru test)?
   ≤2 defining gestures.
6. **Story anchor.** Does the form encode something *specific* (sorya-press ridges, aaji's
   hand/thumbprint, steel dabba lid, bhajani square coil) that copy can name and repeat?
7. **Fixed-form / variable-color readiness.** Silhouette stays identical across the 4 SKU
   spot colours (Blue Tokai model); recognition survives every recolour and 1-ink knockout.
8. **Lockup modularity.** Degrades gracefully down the ladder: bilingual lockup → Latin
   lockup → mark alone, each composition still balanced with pieces removed.
9. **Motion latency.** Has an obvious single-action micro-animation (draw-in, coil, press,
   settle) that finishes <1s and reads as the same brand when frozen mid-motion.
10. **Warmth vs. slickness.** Reads homemade/handcrafted (slight asymmetry, humanist
    curves) not corporate-geometric — but never sloppy. The Burger King "real food, flat and
    crave-able" register, translated to faral.

## 5 refinement recipes

1. **Optical correction of spiral terminals.** Taper the outer terminal over the last
   ~15–20% of the path (width → ~60%) and end on a slight outward *lift*, not a hard chop;
   round the inner terminal into a filled dot/comma so the eye of the spiral doesn't read as
   a hole at small sizes. Rebalance negative gaps between coils to *optically equal* widths
   (outer gaps must be physically wider than inner to look equal).
2. **Ridge quantization.** For the chakli's spiked edge: fix ridge count (≈9–13 per
   revolution), amplitude ≥ stroke-width/3, and **delete ridges from the innermost coil
   entirely** — at 16px inner ridges become mud. Keep ridge rhythm on the outer coil only:
   this is the level-of-detail ladder *inside one mark*.
3. **Shirorekha alignment pass.** Set the shirorekha between Latin small-cap and cap-height
   (or at x-height for a lowercase logotype); scale Devanagari up ~8–15% from equal point
   size until stroke colour matches; check 'o' vs 'क' contrast direction; verify no
   accidental headline breaks; add matra clearance to the clearspace spec. If A3 shares one
   bar for shirorekha + Latin underline, confirm the bar weight suits *both* scripts.
4. **Ink-trap and junction surgery.** At every acute junction (spiral meets wordmark,
   Devanagari knots, letter joins) open a small notch (ink trap) so 1-colour print and 16px
   rendering don't clog; thicken any stroke below ~1/20 of mark height; open counters ~10%
   beyond what looks right at display size.
5. **Weight-ladder mastering.** Produce the mark in 3 *drawn* weights — display (packaging,
   fine detail allowed), standard (web/nav), micro (favicon/embroidery: strokes +15%, detail
   shed, gaps widened) — and ship all three in the kit with switch-over sizes documented.
   Never let one drawing serve all sizes.

## Which family best serves a D2C snack brand — recommendation

**Primary: the spiral mark + bilingual wordmark as one combination system** — the spiral
family (B) carrying the symbol, the wordmark family (A) as its inseparable partner. Reasons:
- D2C snack brands live on **mark-first surfaces**: favicon, Instagram DP, WhatsApp Business
  avatar, pouch roundel, sticker seals — a wordmark-only identity has nothing to put there.
- The category audit shows **no incumbent owns a crafted chakli spiral**; it is the single
  most ownable asset available, *if* de-genericised per §3 (ridges, squared coil, tapered
  terminal, named story — the koru playbook).
- It plugs straight into the existing v2 system (chakli-spiral roundel already on pouch
  labels; draw-in motion already technique #3 in the cookbook) and satisfies the Blue Tokai
  fixed-form / variable-colour SKU model.
- **Lead candidates to refine:** **B1 (Pure Coil)** as the clean master silhouette; **B6
  (Pressed Thumb)** for the strongest story/negative-space double-read (Ubisoft-fingerprint
  precedent = "hand-pressed"); paired with **A6 (Spiral Ligature)** or **A3 (Bilingual
  Twin)** for the wordmark half. B2 (tile) and B3 (sun) are *derived* app-icon/festive
  lockups, not the master. Deprioritise B4/B5 as primaries (colour-dependent / two-part).

**Runner-up: emblem (family C)** — spiral + bilingual type in a roundel — but only as a
*derived lockup* of the primary system (the pouch roundel, the sticker, the wax seal), never
a standalone family. Full-time emblems trap the type at small sizes and fail the favicon
test; C1 (Heritage Roundel) is the best *derived* form, C3/C6's relief/postmark detail is
packaging garnish, not a logo.

**Wordmark (family A):** insufficient *alone* for D2C mark surfaces, but the bilingual
wordmark must be drawn at true logo quality because packaging leads with it (Subko lesson:
the script stack is itself an asset). It is **half of the primary system**, not a competitor
to it — invest the Ek-Type-grade craft here.

**Illustrative (family D):** reject as the *logo*; adopt as a **mascot / illustration
layer** (Amul model: the girl is the campaign device; "Amul" the logotype is the logo). D1
(Aaji Mark) and D2 (Sorya Hands) belong on packs, stories, and topical posts — with the
discipline that the *mark itself never changes*. D's spiral-integrations (D4 peacock-tail
→ coil, D2 chakli from the press) are excellent *illustration* motifs and useful spiral
studies, not scalable marks.

**One-line verdict:** refine a de-genericised chakli **spiral mark (B1/B6)** as the symbol,
lock it to a **craft-grade bilingual wordmark (A3/A6)**, express **emblem C1** only as the
pouch/sticker roundel, and run **illustrative D1/D2** as a fixed-mark, variable-voice mascot
layer.
